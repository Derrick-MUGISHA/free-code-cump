Grace Hopper: The Grandmother of Computing

A Remarkable Life and Legacy

Grace Brewster Murray Hopper was a trailblazing computer scientist, mathematician, and United States Navy rear admiral who made significant contributions to the field of computer programming.

Key Achievements

  • Developed the first compiler for a computer programming language
  • Pioneered the concept of machine-independent programming languages
  • Coined the term "debugging" after removing a moth from a computer
  • Instrumental in the development of COBOL, a high-level programming language

Notable Quotes

"It's easier to ask forgiveness than it is to get permission."
